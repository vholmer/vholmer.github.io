日本語(にほんご) を 話(はな) しましょう！

私(わたし) はビクトルです。 日本語(にほんご) の 大学生(だいがくせい) です。 二十一(にじゅういち) 歳(さい) です。
私(わたし) の 日本語(にほんご) は 悪(わる) いです。すみません！

I have since January 2015 been studying japanese. I chose this language because of its seemingly exotic and different sound, its writing system and also because I've always wanted to learn and east asian language. I am currently following an international specialization within my program (Computer Science), in which studying in Japan will be required. Studying Japanese has so far been a pretty nice pause from the otherwise hectic engineering classes. The fact that I after a few weeks of studying realized that you need to know about 2000 pictograms (so called kanji) to read and understand everyday Japanese put me off just a bit. But I've come to realize that this is no impossible task!

I also happened to discover that it's quite fun to study a language this different from your own. It has led to me actively studying the language on my spare time. However, I strongly believe that one of the biggest mistakes in the history of Japan has been to adopt the chinese writing system. I also believe that I am not alone in feeling this way.
